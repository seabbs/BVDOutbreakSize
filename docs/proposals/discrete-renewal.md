# Proposal: discrete-time renewal architecture

Status: prototype for review (issue #81 structure, branch `arch-renewal`).
Author: drafted by an agent under human oversight.

## Motivation

The current model treats the latent epidemic as a deterministic
continuous-time cumulative-incidence curve $C(s) = \exp(r s)$ from a single
zoonotic seed, with one constant growth rate $r$.
Every observation stream is a thinned, delayed view of that one curve, and
the forward maps are numerical integrals against the exponential.
This is faithful to McCabe et al. but carries two structural assumptions
the limitations section already flags: growth is exponential and constant
for the whole outbreak, and incidence is deterministic rather than a
stochastic process.

A discrete-time renewal process relaxes both.
Latent daily infections are generated by a renewal equation with a
time-varying reproduction number $R_t$, so the growth rate can bend over
the outbreak rather than being pinned to one doubling time.
Delays are applied by discrete convolution on the same daily grid, which
removes the bespoke quadrature integrators and gives a single mechanism
that also accepts time-resolved data when it arrives (issue #52).
This is the project owner's preferred direction.

## Model

### Latent process

Work on a daily grid $t = 1, \dots, n$, with day $n$ the data cut-off $T$
and day $1$ the seeding day.
The outbreak age is therefore $n$ days.

Daily infections follow the renewal equation

$$
I_t = R_t \sum_{s \ge 1} I_{t-s}\, g_s,
\qquad I_1 = I_0,
$$

with $g$ the generation-interval PMF (indexed from lag $1$, so an infectee
is always infected strictly after its infector) and $I_0$ the seeding
infections on day $1$.

The reproduction number evolves as a Gaussian random walk on the log
scale, sampled non-centred to avoid the random-walk funnel,

$$
\log R_1 = \log R_0,
\qquad
\log R_t = \log R_{t-1} + \sigma_{\mathrm{rw}}\, z_{t-1},
\quad z_{t-1} \sim \mathcal N(0, 1).
$$

### From infections to observations

Infections are mapped to symptom onsets by an incubation PMF, then onsets
are mapped to each observed event by its own delay PMF, all by discrete
convolution $(\,y_t = \sum_d x_{t-d}\, \mathrm{delay}_{d}\,)$:

- onset-to-death (Gamma, Isiro 2012 priors) gives expected daily deaths
  after scaling by the CFR;
- onset-to-report gives expected daily reported cases after scaling by the
  DRC ascertainment fraction $p_{\mathrm{DRC}}$;
- onset-to-detection gives the at-risk export person-time, scaled by the
  travel rate $q$ and the Uganda ascertainment fraction $p_{\mathrm{Uganda}}$;
- the export person-time convolved again with onset-to-death gives expected
  deaths among exports.

Expected cumulative counts at $T$ are the sums of the daily series.
The cumulative case count $C(T) = \sum_t I_t$ is the headline quantity.

### How the four streams plus TMRCA map in

| Stream | Mechanism in the renewal model |
|---|---|
| 1. Exports in Uganda | $p_{\mathrm{Uganda}}\, q \sum_t (\text{onset} * \text{detect})_t$, Poisson |
| 2. DRC suspected deaths | $\mathrm{CFR}\sum_t (\text{onset} * \text{o2d})_t$, NegBinomial($k$) |
| 3. DRC reported cases | $p_{\mathrm{DRC}}\sum_t (\text{onset} * \text{report})_t$, NegBinomial($k$) |
| 4. Deaths among exports | $\mathrm{CFR}\, p_{\mathrm{Uganda}}\, q \sum_t (\text{detect} * \text{o2d})_t$, Poisson |
| TMRCA soft bound | $\Phi((n - g)/\sigma)$ added to the log density |

The TMRCA enters exactly as in the current model: a right-censored noisy
reading of the outbreak age, contributing the log of the upper-tail
probability, here a function of the grid length $n$.

### Priors and delays

- $\log R_0 \sim \mathcal N(\log 2, 0.5)$, $\sigma_{\mathrm{rw}} \sim
  \mathcal N^+(0, 0.1)$, innovations $z \sim \mathcal N(0, 1)$.
- $I_0 \sim \mathcal N^+(1, 1)$ (single zoonotic seed).
- Generation interval: Gamma, mean $12$ d, SD $6$ d, fixed (a handful of
  aggregate counts cannot identify it).
- Incubation: Gamma, mean $7$ d. Onset-to-report: mean $5$ d.
  Onset-to-detection: mean $10$ d. All fixed in the prototype.
- Onset-to-death: Gamma with $\alpha \sim \mathcal N^+(4.3, 1.22)$,
  $\theta \sim \mathcal N^+(2.6, 0.82)$ (Isiro 2012, as now).
- $\mathrm{CFR} \sim \mathrm{Beta}(6.6, 13.4)$; $1/\sqrt k \sim
  \mathcal N^+(0.6, 0.2)$; pooled logit ascertainment as now; daily
  travellers $\sim \mathcal N^+(1871, 200)$.

## Mooncake AD findings, including the CensoredDistributions verdict

The blocker the owner flagged is real and concrete.

- **CensoredDistributions.jl installs and runs.** v0.2.15 adds cleanly to a
  Turing/Mooncake environment. `double_interval_censored`,
  `interval_censored` and `primary_censored` all build correct,
  normalised discretised PMFs (verified numerically).
- **The default double-interval-censored Gamma does NOT differentiate
  under Mooncake.** Its analytical primary-censored CDF routes through
  `HypergeometricFunctions.pFqweniger`, which contains a `try/catch`.
  Mooncake rejects try/catch in reverse mode with a
  `MooncakeRuleCompilationError`.
- **`force_numeric = true` does not rescue it.** That path calls QuadGK's
  `cachedrule`, which Mooncake also cannot differentiate.
- **`interval_censored` alone differentiates but returns a NaN
  shape-parameter gradient.** It is built on the Gamma CDF, whose
  shape-parameter derivative is unstable/NaN under Mooncake. This is the
  same limitation the package already documents for the Gamma CDF.

Verdict: CensoredDistributions.jl is not usable with Mooncake for a Gamma
delay today, neither the analytical nor the numeric path.
It would only become viable if (a) `HypergeometricFunctions` were given a
Mooncake `rrule!!` or rewritten without try/catch, or (b) a primary
distribution with an AD-clean analytical CDF were used.
This should be raised upstream rather than worked around silently.

**Fallback that works (and is used here).** Discretise the delay by hand:
integrate the density over each daily bin (trapezoid) and renormalise, with
the density at the origin forced to zero (exact for a Gamma with shape
$> 1$, where the origin's $0\cdot\log 0$ shape derivative would otherwise be
NaN). This touches the density only, never the CDF, and gives **fully
finite Mooncake gradients on both the shape and scale parameters**
(verified). It is the same trick the package already relies on in
`ExportDeathDelay`. See `discretise_delay` in `src/renewal.jl`.

A second AD-stability fix was needed: the renewal recursion can transiently
overflow on extreme NUTS warmup proposals (large $R_t$ compounding),
producing a non-finite expected count. `max(x, eps)` propagates a NaN, so
the expected rates are passed through a `isfinite` guard (`_safe_rate`)
before the Poisson/NegBinomial likelihoods, mirroring `expected_exports`.

## Demonstration

`scripts/renewal_smoke.jl` and `test/test_renewal.jl` show the prototype:

1. compiles and draws from the prior (sensible counts for all four
   streams);
2. yields a finite, nonzero Mooncake gradient of the 101-parameter joint
   log density;
3. completes a short NUTS run (100 iterations, 1 chain) in ~17 s without
   erroring.

## Expected runtime vs current

The current joint fit runs in roughly 6 minutes.
The renewal model replaces the per-call quadrature with $O(n^2)$ renewal
plus $O(n \cdot L)$ convolutions, and the parameter dimension grows from a
dozen to $\sim n$ because of the daily $R_t$ walk (101 parameters at
$n = 90$).
The 100-iteration smoke test took 17 s, dominated by the first-call
gradient compilation; per-gradient cost after warmup is small.
A rough extrapolation puts a 4-chain $\times$ 1000-sample fit in the
single-digit-to-low-tens-of-minutes range, i.e. comparable to or somewhat
slower than the current model, with the cost set mainly by the larger
parameter space and the NUTS trajectory length the $R_t$ walk needs.
This needs confirming with a full fit before any production switch.

## Identifiability risks

- **The daily $R_t$ walk is badly over-parameterised for the data.** Four
  aggregate counts and one soft bound cannot inform $\sim n$ daily
  reproduction numbers; the $R_t$ posterior will track its prior almost
  everywhere and the walk SD $\sigma_{\mathrm{rw}}$ will be weakly
  identified. The headline $C(T)$ may be reasonably constrained while the
  trajectory shape is essentially prior. A coarser $R_t$ (a few segments,
  or a tight prior) is likely more honest than a daily walk here.
- **Grid length $n$ stands in for the outbreak age $T$** and is currently
  fixed. To estimate the age, $n$ (or a continuous start offset) must
  become a parameter, which is awkward on a fixed integer grid; this is the
  main modelling gap versus the current model, where $T$ is continuous and
  sampled.
- **Generation interval and the incubation/report/detection delays are
  fixed.** Freeing them would not be identified by the present data.
- **Stream overlap is unchanged.** The four streams still observe
  overlapping individuals and are treated as conditionally independent, so
  the same double-counting caveat as now applies.

## Drift-from-replication assessment

This architecture deliberately departs from McCabe et al., who invert a
deterministic summary formula at fixed nuisance parameters.
It keeps the same epidemiological inputs (Isiro delay, CFR, travel rate,
ascertainment, TMRCA) and the same four streams, so the priors and data
are comparable, but the latent process is now a stochastic renewal with
time-varying $R_t$ rather than fixed-rate exponential growth.
Headline $C(T)$ estimates should be compared against the current model
before adopting; a divergence would itself be informative about how much
the exponential-growth assumption drives the current numbers.

## Migration effort

- The prototype is additive: `src/renewal.jl` is `include`d by the module
  and exports its own names, leaving the exponential-growth model and all
  existing tests untouched (full suite still passes).
- To productionise: make the outbreak age a parameter; decide on the $R_t$
  resolution; replace the fixed incubation/report/detection delays with
  documented priors; add posterior-summary, plotting and forecast paths
  analogous to the current ones; and add the time-resolved likelihoods
  (issue #52) that the daily grid now makes natural.
- The biggest open question is whether the extra latent flexibility earns
  its keep given the data: with four aggregate counts the daily $R_t$ walk
  is unlikely to be identified, so a reduced-flexibility $R_t$ may be the
  sensible production form.

## Recommendation

The architecture is technically viable end to end under Mooncake, and the
CensoredDistributions blocker has a clean, proven fallback.
It is worth pursuing as the route to time-resolved data (issue #52) and to
relaxing the constant-growth assumption.
But for the present aggregate-only data the full daily $R_t$ walk is
over-parameterised; a production version should use a coarser $R_t$ and
make the outbreak age an explicit parameter before it can replace the
current model.
